How The Free Book Nook works

Book recommendations and collections are created by users. If we don't already have what you're looking for:

  1. You request any novel, manga, cookbook, etc.
  2. We search far and wide to contact the publisher/author
  3. You can permanently read the digital book for free at The Free Book Nook! (Legally!)
  4. Royalties are continuously distributed based on mandatory reader ratings.

Publishers/authors are paid using a combination of ad revenue, donations, sponsors, and grants.

About Us

We're a (soon to be) 501(c)(3) nonprofit aiming to make free and legal e-books available to everyone, anywhere in the world, while compensating creators. We aim to eliminate monetary or geographical barriers for readers.

Why The Free Book Nook matters

Books are rising in price, motivating consumers toward shadow libraries. For readers that acquire titles through legal means, very little of the purchase itself goes to the author/publisher, and large corporations take most of the profit. Libraries---although fantastic for reading and building community---are not available to everyone and may not be able to acquire every title due to limited physical space.

We operate without a profit incentive, so the majority of money goes directly to authors/publishers. Only a small portion is used to maintain our services. This makes our system extremely cost-effective.

FAQ

How is this different from my local library?

Your local library probably has a good selection of books, and perhaps events or community spaces. Go visit them!

However, obtaining new materials is often expensive for libraries. Additionally: they have limited physical space so can only acquire so many books at a time, some books are not in certain library systems, and some people may not have a library or the closest library may be too far. A digital reading space has less of these limitations, while offering more convenience and flexibility to readers.

We acknowledge that e-books just aren't the same as print, and encourage you to visit your local library! That's why we plan on implementing a link so you can search for a title's availability in libraries near you.

Who can upload to The Free Book Nook?

From any country, any book or academic paper with an ISBN or DOI, respectively, can be uploaded by certain people. Note that we expect low readership for academic papers.

  1. Author/assistant
    1. Self published author: please make an author account and upload your book(s) directly. Thank you for your contribution!
    2. Author with publisher: first check with your publisher contract to ensure that you have the rights to distribute your book through regular distribution channels (online marketplaces), and if your publisher then receives a portion. Then, create your author account and upload your book! You are responsible for adhering to the terms of your contract(s) and distributing income from The Free Book Nook as stated by your contract. If your publisher contacts us regarding an unauthorized upload, we will remove the content and discontinue income streams until favorable terms are found by all parties.
    3. Author's assistant: if you are an assistant or similar role to complete actions on the author's behalf, feel free to upload the book in accordance to the Author with publisher guidelines.
  2. Publisher/distribution entity
    1. Publisher: please make a Publisher account and upload the book(s) on behalf of an author. The publisher is responsible for ensuring income reaches the author(s) in accordance to publisher contracts. If an author contacts The Free Book Nook regarding an unauthorized upload or lost income, we will remove the content and discontinue income streams until favorable terms are found by all parties.
    2. Distribution entity: a distribution entity may not be legally allowed to upload content. Please check with the publisher for permission and contract details. Regardless of legality, we aim to compensate publishers/authors, not separate distribution entities. We welcome any new books added to The Free Book Nook, as long as the initial publishers/authors are the only recipients of monetary compensation.
How much do publishers/authors get paid?

Typically, you can expect to get around 0.3-3 USD* per read. This is standard for most online sales (ex Amazon), although we expect demand to be higher due to the books being free.

Each payment is calculated based on number of words in the book, number of previous reads in a time frame, and user ratings. For example, a 70k word book read for the first time in its entirety and rated "good" would get about 1.5 USD, whereas the thousandth reader that month who happened to give the same rating would earn the creator(s) less. This is intended to mimic bulk pricing in the book market.

Why donate to The Free Book Nook?

Every donation has a direct and large impact due to our cost-effective system. Even around 5 USD will fund an average of around 3 reads of a full-length novel, or around 6 novellas.

Because we don't aim to make a profit, we only require enough money to roughly meet what publishers/authors would have earned from sales, which is often around 0.3-2 USD* per book for US based authors, depending on book type (paperback, e-book, etc.). Additionally, distributing e-books drastically reduces operating costs as compared to a physical location.
